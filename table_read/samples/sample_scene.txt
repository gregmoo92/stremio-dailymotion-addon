INT. DINER - NIGHT

Rain hammers the windows. A near-empty room. NEON light bleeds across cracked vinyl booths.

MAYA (28), eyes red-rimmed, nurses a coffee. Across from her, DET. KOWALSKI (50s), trench coat dripping, slides into the booth.

KOWALSKI
You shouldn't have come.

MAYA
You said you had answers.

KOWALSKI
(quiet)
I said I had questions.

Maya looks away. Outside, a car passes too slowly.

MAYA
Then ask them.

KOWALSKI
Where were you the night your sister disappeared?

A long beat. The waitress, ROSIE (60s), refills Kowalski's mug without being asked.

ROSIE
Pie's fresh, hon.

KOWALSKI
(without looking up)
Not tonight, Rosie.

Rosie shuffles off. Maya's hands tremble around her cup.

MAYA
I was at the lake.

KOWALSKI
Alone?

MAYA
(barely audible)
She was already gone when I got there.

Kowalski leans back. Studies her. The rain gets louder.

KOWALSKI
Then why'd you wait six years to say so?

FADE OUT.
